The Reception Reality

Every dental practice knows the feeling. The phone rings while reception is checking in a patient, processing a payment and fielding a question about parking. By the time anyone reaches the phone, the caller has gone.

Research suggests that 20 to 40 percent of calls to dental practices go unanswered during peak times. Lunch hours are particularly challenging, with reduced cover and higher call volumes colliding at exactly the wrong moment.

New patient enquiries are often the first calls abandoned. These callers rarely leave voicemails. They simply ring the next practice on the list. Every missed call is a missed opportunity, and once that patient books elsewhere, the lifetime value walks with them.

Cancellations create another pressure point. Late notice gaps in the diary are difficult to fill when reception is already stretched. And once the practice closes for the day, out-of-hours calls go entirely unanswered, even from patients who would happily book an early morning or evening appointment.

The result is a practice that is busy, professional and well run, but quietly losing revenue through calls that simply never get answered.
