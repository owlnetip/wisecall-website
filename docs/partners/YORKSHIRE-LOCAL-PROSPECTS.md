## How to get director emails (ethical, practical)

Do **not** buy scraped lists. Use public sources only:

| Step | Source | What you get |
|------|--------|--------------|
| 1 | Company **website Contact / About** | `hello@` / `info@` / named founder email |
| 2 | **LinkedIn** — founder/director profile | Often lists email in About; else InMail |
| 3 | **Companies House** | Officer names (directors) — then guess patterns |
| 4 | Email pattern tools (Hunter, Apollo, etc.) | `firstname@domain` after you know the domain |
| 5 | Phone → ask for director | “Who looks after partnerships?” |

**Common UK SME patterns** (verify before mass-sending):

- `hello@` / `info@` / `enquiries@` (start here — often reaches owners at small firms)
- `firstname@domain.co.uk`
- `firstname.lastname@domain.co.uk`

**Rule:** Prefer **named founder + company email** over guessed personal Gmail. For 1–10 person firms, `hello@` *is* the director’s inbox.

## Compliance reminder

- Only use **publicly published** business emails for B2B partnership outreach.
- Include unsubscribe / clear identity (WiseCall, Wetherby).
- Don’t scrape personal Gmail/Hotmail from data dumps.
- PECR/GDPR: B2B soft opt-in for similar products is generally OK for corporate addresses — keep messages relevant and infrequent.
