## 1. Honest audit of the current site

### What is working (keep it)
- **The positioning bones are already right.** The current hero — "Every call answered. Every enquiry captured." — plus the subheadline "the AI front desk for UK businesses… answers in your business name… sends your team a clear summary" is genuinely close to the target positioning. This is not a repositioning project; it's an execution-quality project.
- **The tap-to-call / call-me-back demo is the site's best asset.** Real number (+44 113 522 2277), two interaction modes (dial out, get called back). Almost no competitor lets you talk to the product 30 seconds after landing. It's currently under-staged, but the mechanism is there.
- **The 5-step call story exists** (Answer → Qualify → Capture → Book or route → Summarise) and it's the right story.
- **Dental is already correctly concentrated on `/dental`**, with a strong dental hero ("Never miss another patient enquiry"), Dentally booking claims, a dental calculator, pilot programme, and dental FAQ. The homepage treats dental as one industry card among five. The brief's biggest structural worry is already satisfied.
- **Industry pages exist and are specific** (dental, legal, property, trades, care) with real per-industry pain ("Capture every lead while you are on a job"), not templated filler.
- **Trust section exists** (UK infrastructure, audit trail, access controls, GDPR-aware) and email-channel and partner sections are present.

### What is weak (fix it)
1. **Two-tier site quality.** The homepage and industry pages are real marketing pages. But `/pricing`, `/how-it-works`, `/compare/*`, `/resources/missed-call-calculator`, and `/industries` are thin SEO-template pages: all share an identical "What WiseCall does on every call" block and a "Priority Pages" footer, and `/industries` publicly says **"Need an industry page built next?"** — internal planning language leaking to buyers. A prospect who clicks Pricing from the polished homepage falls off a quality cliff at the exact moment of highest intent.
2. **Plan-name inconsistency (revenue-affecting) — FIXED 2026-07-01.** Homepage sold **Starter £99 / Professional £199 / Business £399**; the generated `/pricing` page showed **Core / Growth / Pro** with no prices at all. Checked the source of truth (`apps/portal/src/lib/stripe.ts`): `PlanId = "starter" | "professional" | "business"` is the live, current Stripe billing model; `Core/Growth/Pro` is explicitly typed `LegacyPlanId`, kept only so old subscriptions keep working. So the homepage was already correct — `/pricing` was the stale page. Rebuilt `renderPricing()` in `scripts/generate-seo-pages.mjs` to match the homepage exactly (Starter/Professional/Business, real prices, allowances, Enterprise tier, honest 7-day-pilot-then-12-month-term wording). Verified via `npm run generate:seo && npm run build`.
3. ~~The pricing page has no prices.~~ Fixed in the same pass — real £ figures now render on `/pricing`.
4. **No product mockups.** The site tells the story in text and step cards but never *shows* a call summary, an enquiry card, a routing rule, or the dashboard. For a product whose output is "a clean summary and a next step," not showing that artefact is the biggest missed persuasion opportunity.
5. **Visual identity is one-note.** Dark teal (#172929) with a single cyan accent (#7de8eb) everywhere — 351 uses of the accent colour on the homepage alone. It reads "developer dark mode" rather than "premium, calm front desk." Emoji icons (🦷 🏛️ 🏠 🔧 🏡) on industry cards undercut the premium bar.
6. **Demo is framed as a feature, not a magic moment.** "Hear it for yourself" is buried mid-hero with no scenario framing, no expectation-setting ("60 seconds, no signup"), and no post-call payoff (you never see the summary the call generated — which is the whole product).
7. **No comparison content strategy.** One thin comparison page exists; voicemail / virtual receptionist / hiring comparisons — the highest-intent searches in this category — are missing.
8. **Social proof is absent.** No testimonials, no numbers, no logos. Understandable at this stage, but the site doesn't yet have a proof *system* ready to receive them.

## 2. Positioning strategy

**Positioning statement:**
> WiseCall is the AI front desk for UK service businesses. It answers every call in your name, captures what matters, books or routes the next step, and hands your team a clear summary — so no enquiry is ever lost to voicemail, hold music, or a busy front desk.

**Messaging hierarchy (homepage):**
1. What it is: an AI front desk that answers every enquiry (5-second comprehension)
2. Proof you can feel: call it right now
3. The cost of the status quo: missed calls are silent revenue leaks
4. How it works: the 5-step call story, shown as product
5. Who it's for: industry cards (dental one of six)
6. Can we trust it: humans stay in control, rules, audit trail
7. What changes: before/after outcomes
8. What it costs: three clear plans + 7-day pilot
9. Act: pilot / demo / call the AI

**Differentiation:** vs voicemail (nobody leaves one), vs answering services (scripts + message-taking, no structure, per-minute pricing), vs hiring (£24k+/yr for 40 hrs/week vs 24/7), vs generic AI bots (no business rules, no escalation, no audit trail). WiseCall's wedge: **structured outcomes** — every call ends as a summary + next step your team can act on, and you can **test it on real calls in a 7-day pilot** before committing.

**Why now:** customers stopped tolerating voicemail; AI call handling crossed the reliability threshold; UK service businesses can't hire reception staff. The calm framing: *"AI that calmly handles the chaos."*

## 4. Homepage redesign

**§1 Hero.** Keep the winning headline, raise the staging.
- H1: **"Every call answered. Every enquiry captured."** (Alternates tested and rejected: "The AI front desk that answers every enquiry" — flatter; "Never lose another customer to voicemail" — negative-first; the current line is the strongest.)
- Sub: "WiseCall is the AI front desk for UK service businesses. It answers in your name, captures what matters, books or routes the next step, and sends your team a clear summary."
- CTAs: **[Try it now]** primary (per CTA canon) · **[Call the demo]** loud tap-to-call · "Book a demo" text link.
- Visual: a live call-to-summary mockup — phone ringing → "WiseCall answering as *Bright Smiles Dental*" → transcript snippets → a summary card materialising (caller, reason, urgency, next step, assigned to). This *is* the product story in one image.
- Trust strip beneath: UK-based · GDPR-aware · Human escalation built in · No phone system required.

**§2 Problem.** "The calls you never knew you missed." Four sharp cards: reception is busy with the person in front of them · 80% of callers won't leave a voicemail · out-of-hours enquiries vanish silently · nobody can see what was missed. One line of maths: "At 10 missed calls a week and £200 an enquiry, that's £2,000/week leaking — invisibly." Link to calculator.

**§3 Product story.** Upgrade the existing 5 steps into an interactive **call timeline**: Answer → Understand → Capture → Act (book / route / escalate / message) → Summarise, each step paired with a product mockup state. Keep the existing industry-toggle (Dental/Legal/Property/Trades/Care) — it's a great pattern; give each toggle a real example transcript + summary.

**§4 Live demo — the magic moment.** Full-width section, visually distinct (light panel on the dark site — see §10 visual direction).
- Header: **"Don't take our word for it. Call it."**
- Scenario picker: New enquiry · Existing customer · Out-of-hours message · Urgent escalation · Appointment request.
- Two actions: tap-to-call the demo number, or "enter your mobile and the agent calls you" (existing mechanism).
- Expectation microcopy: "Takes about 60 seconds. No signup. It's a real WiseCall agent."
- **The payoff:** after (or beside) the call, show "here's the summary WiseCall just produced" — even a simulated one on desktop. The summary artefact is the conversion moment.

**§5 Use cases.** Outcome-led card grid: missed-call handling, out-of-hours, new enquiry capture, urgent escalation, reception overflow, multi-location visibility, lead qualification, follow-up. Each card = pain → what WiseCall does → outcome, one line each.

**§6 Industries.** Six equal cards (Dental, Legal, Property, Trades, Care, Multi-site), each: main pain / how WiseCall helps / outcome / link. Replace emoji with a consistent line-icon set. Dental links to `/dental` — one strong card, not the narrative.

**§7 Trust & control.** "Your team stays in control. WiseCall handles the noise." Pillars: follows business-approved rules · escalates to humans when unsure · every call logged, transcribed, auditable · you can review and change behaviour any time · sensitive workflows can be restricted. Keep existing UK-infrastructure/GDPR/access-controls content, merged here. Confident tone, not defensive.

**§8 Outcomes.** Before/after module. Before: voicemails, rushed notes, invisible missed calls, interrupted staff. After: every enquiry answered, structured summaries, clear next steps, manager visibility. Frame as "what changes in week one of a pilot."

**§9 Product preview.** 3–4 believable mockups: unified call log/inbox · a single call summary with urgency flag and assigned follow-up · a routing rule ("emergencies → ring Sarah's mobile") · the insights view (call volumes, reasons, out-of-hours demand — the AI Insights dashboard already exists in the product; show it).

**§10 Pricing teaser.** Three cards, **Starter / Professional / Business** (the live Stripe plan family — matches homepage + /pricing as of 2026-07-01), real prices, "best for" labels, "every plan includes" line, 7-day pilot banner, CTA to /pricing. (PAYG exists as a Stripe product but was scrapped from the portal 2026-06-19 — do not resurface it.)

**§11 Comparison strip.** One elegant table row set: WiseCall vs voicemail vs answering service vs hiring — instant answer / 24/7 / structured summaries / routing / visibility / try-before-committing. Links to full compare pages.

**§12 Proof.** Design the system now, fill as proof arrives: a stats band (calls answered, enquiries captured, avg. answer time — from real platform data), one customer quote slot, one reception-team quote slot, pilot-results card ("what a 7-day pilot showed practice X"). Clearly-labelled placeholders until real; never fake.

**§13 Final CTA.** "See what your business is missing." [Try it now] [Book a demo]. One line beneath: "Set up in a day. No phone system changes. Prove it on real calls in a 7-day pilot."

## 9. Copywriting system

**Tone:** clear, confident, specific, calm, commercially sharp, UK-flavoured, human. Short sentences. Concrete nouns (voicemail, reception desk, callback, summary) over abstractions.

**Banned:** revolutionise, unlock the power of AI, seamless, transform your customer experience, next-generation, cutting-edge, leverage, enhance operational efficiency, "solutions."

**House lines:**
- "When your team is busy, WiseCall answers."
- "No voicemail. No missed details. No caller left hanging."
- "Every call ends with a clear summary and a next step."
- "Your team stays in control. WiseCall handles the noise."
- "Try it on real calls before committing."

**CTA system (canon, confirmed 2026-07-01):** Primary: **Try it now** (app signup). Secondary: **Book a demo**. Industry + pricing pages add: **Start a 7-day pilot**. Supporting links: View pricing · Calculate your missed-call cost · Compare WiseCall · See the dental solution. "Learn more" only on tertiary links. Never: Start Free Trial, Try the AI now, cancel-anytime claims.

**Trust copy pattern:** state the rule, then the mechanism. "WiseCall never gives clinical advice — emergency calls follow your practice-approved escalation rules and every step is logged."

## 10. Visual direction

**Feeling:** "AI that calmly handles the chaos." Premium, calm, warm-but-precise.

- **Palette evolution, not revolution:** keep the deep teal (#172929) as the brand anchor but stop drowning everything in cyan. Introduce a **warm off-white "daylight" mode for content sections** (problem, pricing, trust, FAQ) so the site alternates dark brand moments with light readable ones — this alone will lift perceived quality more than anything else (light-sections.css already exists; extend it). Cyan (#7de8eb) demoted to *the* accent: CTAs, live-state pulses, key data. Add one warm neutral (soft sand) for summary-card artefacts so "the output" is visually distinct.
- **Typography:** Inter stays for UI/body; add a distinctive display face for headlines (e.g. a modern grotesk with character) so headings stop looking default.
- **Motifs:** waveform → structured summary card (the signature transition); missed call (red dot) → captured opportunity (filled card); calls flowing through a calm routing layer. No robots, no glow blobs, no headsets, no emoji icons — replace with a single consistent stroke-icon set.
- **Mockups:** one believable product-UI style used everywhere (call log, summary card, routing rule, insights). Real-looking data, UK names, UK numbers.
- **Motion:** calm and purposeful — demo pulse on the call button, summary card assembling line-by-line, counter animations in the calculator, gentle FAQ/card reveals. 200–300ms, ease-out, nothing bouncy.
