WISECALL The approach

One layer, not five tools

You asked suppliers to think beyond the email inbox, and to show how AI could work across the whole business rather than fixing one thing in isolation. We think that is exactly the right instinct, and it is the only way the individual pieces compound instead of competing.

GenetiQ stays what it should be: the single source of truth for stock, prices, orders and ledgers. What an ERP cannot do is hold a conversation, with a designer in New York at nine in the evening, with a mill that has gone quiet on a delivery date, or with an account that has slowly stopped ordering. That is what the AI layer does. Every conversation the business has, on any channel, runs through agents that read and act on GenetiQ, and everything they learn is written back into it.

WISECALL Your ERP position

The migration is the opportunity

You are moving from Reflex Mertex, a system built specifically for textiles, to GenetiQ, a strong general merchant and distribution ERP. Your IT partner has the build done and the full API package is licensed. Three things follow from that, and they shape this entire plan.

1

Nothing here is blocked

The usual first hurdle, waiting months for API access, does not exist for you. We can connect on day one and start with sales rather than with integration groundwork.

2

The trade knowledge needs a home

A general ERP handles stock and ledgers superbly. The textile specifics you had in Mertex, dye lots, piece and cut logic, sampling and cutting workflows, tend to need somewhere to live. The AI layer is a natural home for that expertise.

3

History is worth capturing now

Migration is the one moment your full sales history is being handled anyway. That history is what powers demand forecasting, dormant account detection and segmentation later.

4

Adoption risk cuts both ways

A new ERP means new screens for your team. An AI layer that answers questions in plain English over the top of it softens exactly that transition rather than adding to it.

The point about dye lots, in one line

Colour lives in the batch, and no general ERP will volunteer that on your behalf. Our agent already does: ask it for forty metres of Orissa Silk 31446 and it tells you the stock spans two dye batches, offers a cutting for approval and holds the fabric for seven days, which is your own published policy. That is the difference between software that stores your data and software that understands your trade.

WISECALL Area 02 · Sales

Phase one

Every quote raised, chased and closed

In a fabric house the quote is rarely the hard part. The follow through is. Cuttings go out, books go out, and quotes go quiet. The sales layer treats every quote and sample as a live file in GenetiQ with an owner and a next action, then does the chasing itself: politely, on schedule, in the customer's language, currency and time zone, and stopping the moment one of your team picks it up.

Inbound order emails also stop being retyping work. The agent reads the designer's email, resolves fabric names to quality codes, checks stock and batch, drafts the sales order in GenetiQ, and routes only the genuine judgement calls to your team.

WISECALL Area 04 · Purchasing

Phase three

Reorders decided by evidence

Buying handwoven silk is a long game. Mill lead times run in weeks, and a stockout in a bestselling colourway is revenue you never see. The layer watches sales velocity against piece level stock and lead time for each quality and colourway, proposes reorders while there is still runway, and handles the tedious half of supplier management: chasing confirmations and delivery dates in writing, on schedule.

Because it understands batches, it also protects colour continuity. It knows when the last full piece of a colour is about to be cut, and when incoming stock will not match the remnant already on your shelf.

WISECALL Area 05 · Accounts and export

Phase four

Credit control with perfect manners

Most of an accounts inbox is questions the ERP can already answer: copy invoices, statements, what is outstanding, remittance advice. The agent answers those directly, in whichever language the customer writes in. What is left is the real work, which is judgement on credit and the relationships behind it.

Chasing runs as a sequence tuned by ageing bucket and account history, always accurate to the ledger, and stopping the instant a dispute is raised or a payment lands. Your rules are built in: pro forma markets, credit limits, the 3% card charge above £5,000, and no order release on a stopped account.

One memory across every conversation
